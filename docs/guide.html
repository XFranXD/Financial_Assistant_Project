RESEARCH GUIDE

How to read reports, what each signal means, and what this system cannot tell you.

1. How This System Works

This system runs three times per trading day (10:30, 12:30, 14:30 ET) and once at close (16:10 ET). Each run collects news from free RSS feeds, fetches market data from Yahoo Finance and similar free-tier sources, runs a 20-module analytical pipeline, and ranks companies against each other using a composite confidence score.

No paid data sources are used. No predictions are made. No trades are taken. The output is a ranked list of screening opportunities for further human research.

3. Confidence Score

The confidence score is a composite 0–100 metric. It is NOT a probability of profit. It measures how many independent signals align and how strongly.

85–100
Strong alignment across all signals. Prioritise for deeper research.
70–84
Most signals favorable. Verify key factors before acting.
60–69
Passes screening. Needs additional research.
Below 60
Weak signal. Approach with significant caution.

The score has three sub-components shown in the breakdown: Risk (0–100, lower is safer), Opp (opportunity score, 0–100), Agree (0–100, how many signals agree).

4. Trend Signals (1M / 3M / 6M)

Trend shows price return over 1 month, 3 months, and 6 months. All three positive = confirmed momentum across timeframes. Only one timeframe positive = early-stage or single-period event, not confirmed.

▲ all threeSustained uptrend. Strongest trend signal.
▲ 1M onlyRecent pop. Could be mean reversion or news reaction — verify.
▼ all threeSustained downtrend. Risk of continued decline even with good fundamentals.

5. Valuation Signals

Profit Margin
Cents kept per $1 of revenue. Higher = more efficient. Compare within the same sector.
ROIC
Return on Invested Capital. Above 15% = excellent. 8–15% = solid. Below 8% = weak business economics.
Debt-to-Equity (D/E)
Ratio of debt to shareholder equity. Below 0.3 = very little debt. Above 1.5 = elevated. Above 2.5 = high.
FCF (Operating Cash Flow)
Free Cash Flow proxy. Positive = business generates real cash. Negative = burning cash.

6. Signal Agreement

Signal Agreement measures how many independent analysis modules reach the same directional conclusion. The model is multiplicative: one contradictory signal significantly reduces the overall score. An agreement score of 70+ means most of the 20 analysis modules agree.

7. Volume Signals

Elevated bullishHigh volume on up days — institutional buying signal.
Elevated bearishHigh volume on down days — distribution or panic signal.
Unusually highVolume ≥ 2x average. Something is drawing attention. Investigate why.
Low activityVolume < 0.8x average. Less conviction behind any price move.

8. Risk Score

Composite 0–100 risk measure. Lower is safer.

Debt (D/E)
High leverage amplifies losses in downturns.
Volatility (Beta)
High beta = larger price swings in both directions.
Liquidity
Low average daily volume = harder to exit large positions quickly.
EPS stability
Inconsistent earnings make valuation unreliable.
Drawdown
Large distance from 90-day high signals price weakness.
Margin
Thin margins provide less buffer against cost shocks.

9. Drawdown

Drawdown measures how far the current price is below the 90-day high. A 20%+ drawdown is flagged as a warning even if other metrics look strong — it means the stock has already fallen significantly and the catalyst may have already passed, or downward pressure is ongoing.

10. What This System Cannot Do

Honest free-tier ceiling:

Free-tier APIs provide delayed, sampled, or limited-depth data. This system cannot access real-time tick data, institutional order flow, insider filings, earnings call transcripts, or premium fundamental databases. All values are best-effort approximations that may be stale by hours.

This system cannot predict whether a stock will go up or down. It identifies screening opportunities for further human research only.

Not investment advice. Free-tier data only. Always verify findings through independent sources before making any financial decision. Past system signals are not indicative of future performance.

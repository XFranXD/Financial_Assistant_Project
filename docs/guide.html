How to read scores and act on signals
System Architecture
System 1 · Research Engine System 2 · Earnings Quality System 3 · Sector Rotation
Three integrated systems analyze the market multiple times per trading day. System 1 aggregates news, momentum, and fundamentals into a composite confidence score. System 2 reads SEC filings to assess earnings trustworthiness. System 3 detects active sector rotation. All three feed the final candidate ranking.

Key Terms
Composite Confidence
Risk 35% + Opportunity 35% + Signal Agreement 20% + Sector Momentum 10%.
Signal Agreement
Multiplicative: momentum × sector × event × volume. All four must align.
EQ Verdict
Supportive / Neutral / Weak / Risky / Unavailable. Mapped from SEC filing analysis.
Rotation Signal
Support / Wait / Weaken. Backward-looking — confirms rotation in progress, not a prediction.
EQ Alignment
ALIGNED / PARTIAL / CONFLICT. How well EQ verdict agrees with the market signal.
VIX Regime
Low / Normal / Elevated / Crisis. Crisis reduces confidence in all signals.
Unusual Volume
Flagged at 2× the 30-day average. Adds weight to signal agreement score.
OCF Filter
Positive operating cash flow required. Negative OCF = excluded always.
Rank Board
Resets each Monday. Best confidence score for each ticker during the week is retained.
Free-tier Delay
yFinance data has up to 15-minute delays for price and up to 1-day delay for filing data.
Breadth Label
Derived from the ratio of advancing to declining issues. Weak breadth = broad market pressure.
System Limitations
Does not execute trades, recommend sizes, set stop-losses, provide targets, track positions, or access any brokerage. All outputs are research signals for human review only. Free-tier data has 15-minute delays. Always verify independently before making any investment decision.

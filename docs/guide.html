01 — How the System Works

This is a zero-cost automated stock research engine. It runs four times per trading day — 9:45, 11:45, 13:45, and 16:00 ET — and can also be triggered manually for research on specific tickers. No paid APIs, no machine learning, no trading logic. It collects public data, scores it, and produces a ranked list of screening candidates for human decision-making.

The system has three analytical layers that run in sequence on every candidate:

System 1 — Market Signal
Scores momentum, trend quality, volume, risk, and financial health. Produces a confidence score and a verdict.
System 2 — Earnings Quality
Pulls SEC EDGAR filings to measure whether earnings are cash-backed and sustainable across 7 financial modules.
System 3 — Sector Rotation
Measures sector-level momentum and timing to determine whether capital flow supports or opposes entering now.

After all three systems score a candidate, a Combined Reading synthesises the three signals into an alignment verdict and a conclusion. The final output is a full report with an AI Research Prompt — a pre-built block you paste into any AI assistant for entry and exit analysis.

Who makes the final decision? The system never tells you to buy or sell. It identifies candidates that pass screening thresholds and organises all relevant data so you can make an informed decision, assisted by the AI prompt layer.

02 — System 1: Market Signal Analysis

System 1

System 1 is the primary screening layer. It evaluates each candidate across momentum, trend stability, volume behaviour, financial health, and risk. The output is a confidence score (0–100) and a market verdict.

Verdict Labels

RESEARCH NOW Confidence ≥ 70 and risk ≤ 35. Strong multi-signal alignment. Highest priority for further research.
WATCH Confidence ≥ 55, or low risk with confidence ≥ 45. Partial signals. Worth monitoring for improvement.
SKIP Signals too weak or conflicting. Not worth research time at this stage.

Confidence Score

A composite 0–100 metric. Not a probability of profit. Measures how many independent signals align and how strongly. Three sub-components:

Risk (0–100)
Lower is safer. Composite of debt, volatility, liquidity, EPS stability, drawdown, and margin.
Opportunity (0–100)
Higher is better. Measures trend strength, momentum, and relative opportunity vs. current market regime.
Signal Agreement (0–100)
How many of the 20 analysis modules agree on direction. Multiplicative — one contradiction significantly reduces the score.
85–100
Strong alignment. Highest priority.
70–84
Most signals favorable. Verify before acting.
60–69
Passes screening. Needs additional research.
Below 60
Weak signal. Approach with significant caution.

Trend (1M / 3M / 6M)

Price return over 1, 3, and 6 months. All three positive = confirmed momentum across timeframes.

▲ all threeSustained uptrend across all timeframes. Strongest trend signal.
▲ 1M onlyRecent move only. Could be news reaction — not a confirmed trend.
▼ all threeSustained downtrend. Risk of continued decline even with good fundamentals.

Timing Quality

Trend stage is classified from the 3-month return. This is critical — a stock in a late-stage trend is a worse entry than a weaker stock in an early-stage trend, even if all other signals look good.

EARLY TREND3M return < 15%. Momentum building. Best entry window.
MID TREND3M return 15–30%. Trend established and intact. Acceptable entry.
LATE TREND3M return > 30%. Elevated pullback risk. Requires strong justification from all other signals to enter.

Volume

Elevated bullishHigh volume on up days — institutional buying signal.
Elevated bearishHigh volume on down days — distribution or panic signal.
Unusually highVolume ≥ 2× average. Something is drawing attention — investigate why.
Low activityVolume < 0.8× average. Less conviction behind any price move.

Financial Health

Profit Margin
Cents kept per $1 of revenue. Compare within the same sector only.
ROIC
Return on Invested Capital. Above 15% = excellent. 8–15% = solid. Below 8% = weak business economics.
Debt-to-Equity
Below 0.3 = very little debt. Above 1.5 = elevated. Above 2.5 = high leverage risk.
Operating Cash Flow
Positive = business generates real cash. Negative = burning cash regardless of reported earnings.
Drawdown
Distance from 90-day high. Above 20% flagged as warning — price has already fallen significantly.

Risk Score

Composite 0–100. Lower is safer. Combines debt, volatility (beta), liquidity, EPS stability, drawdown, and margin. Risk ≤ 40 = LOW RISK section. Above 40 = MODERATE RISK section.

03 — System 2: Earnings Quality Analysis

System 2

System 2 pulls raw financial data directly from SEC EDGAR filings and measures whether a company's earnings are reliable, cash-backed, and sustainable. It does not use estimated or screened data — only filed quarterly reports. A minimum of 6 quarters is required to run.

EQ Score

A composite 0–100 score across 7 modules. Higher = more reliable earnings. Accompanied by a pass tier and a data reliability score reflecting how many SEC quarters were available.

Pass Tier

PASS — SUPPORTIVE Earnings are reliable and cash-backed. Fundamentals support the market signal.
WATCH — NEUTRAL Earnings are acceptable but have concerns. Not a blocker, but worth monitoring.
FAIL — WEAK Earnings are unreliable. Reduces conviction significantly regardless of market signal.
FATAL FLAW — RISKY Critical structural problem detected. Overrides all other tiers. Avoid.
UNAVAILABLE No SEC data found. Treat as unvalidated — do not assume positive or negative.

The 7 Modules

Accruals
Gap between reported earnings and actual cash flow. Large positive accruals = earnings may not represent real cash.
Cash Conversion
How consistently earnings convert to operating cash. Low conversion = earnings quality concern.
Revenue Quality
Revenue growth consistency and relationship to receivables. Receivables growing faster than revenue = warning.
FCF Sustainability
Free cash flow vs. capex and investment phase. Measures whether cash generation is sustainable.
Earnings Consistency
EPS stability across quarters. High variance = unreliable earnings base for valuation.
Long-Term Trends
Multi-year momentum in operating cash flow, margins, and capex. Detects structural improvement or deterioration.
Dividend Stability
For dividend-paying stocks: consistency and coverage ratio. A dividend cut triggers the RISKY fatal flaw override.
Why UNAVAILABLE is common EDGAR requires matching ticker to CIK, then finding correct XBRL tags across multiple filing types. Some tickers have incomplete filings, non-standard tags, or insufficient quarterly history. When data is unavailable, System 2 is skipped entirely — it does not penalise the candidate, but it also cannot validate it.

04 — System 3: Sector Rotation Analysis

System 3

System 3 measures whether a candidate's sector is currently receiving capital inflows or outflows. A strong stock in a deteriorating sector faces a headwind. A strong stock in an accelerating sector has a tailwind. Timing matters as much as stock quality.

Rotation Score

A 0–100 score for the candidate's sector ETF. Built from three sub-signals: momentum (price trend of the ETF), relative strength (sector vs. broader market), and volume (capital flow intensity into the sector).

Timing Signal

SUPPORTSector flow supports acting now. Capital is rotating in. Timing is favorable.
WAITNeutral timing. Not yet attracting strong inflows. A better entry may come.
WEAKENSector flow is deteriorating. Capital is rotating out. Timing risk is elevated.
UNKNOWNInsufficient data to determine sector timing.

Commodity Relationship

For commodity-sensitive sectors (energy, materials), the system also tracks 5-day commodity price moves. If the sector is rising while the underlying commodity (crude oil, natural gas) is falling, this is a negative divergence — sector strength may not be sustainable. This is shown in the market narrative block and flagged in the AI prompt.

05 — Combined Reading

Synthesis Layer

After all three systems score a candidate, the Combined Reading synthesises them into a single alignment verdict and a deterministic conclusion sentence.

Alignment

ALIGNEDAll three systems agree. Highest conviction setup.
PARTIALMixed signals — two systems agree, one does not. Proceed with caution.
CONFLICTSystems disagree. The conflict must be understood before any action.

Earnings Contribution to Combined Reading

SUPPORTIVEEQ PASS — earnings validate the market signal.
NEUTRALEQ WATCH — earnings are acceptable, not a blocker.
WEAKEQ FAIL — earnings undermine the market signal.
RISKYEQ fatal flaw — overrides everything. Avoid.
UNAVAILABLENo SEC data. Cannot validate — treat as unconfirmed.

Decision Priority Order

When signals conflict, the system and the AI prompt follow this hierarchy:

1. Timing quality (trend stage) — highest priority 2. Market verdict (System 1) — primary directional signal 3. Sector rotation (System 3) — confirms or weakens timing 4. Earnings quality (System 2) — validates or blocks conviction
Why timing outranks everything A stock can have perfect earnings and strong sector rotation but still be a poor entry if the trend is already extended. LATE TREND (3M > 30%) cannot result in a BUY NOW recommendation regardless of other signals. The maximum allowed entry when timing is late is WAIT.

06 — The AI Research Prompt

AI Layer

Every run generates an AI Research Prompt — a structured block of text containing all automated research data for every candidate in that run. You copy it and paste it into any AI assistant (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) to get entry analysis, exit logic, risk assessment, and a final verdict. No additional instructions are needed — the prompt is self-contained.

This is the last 5% of the process. The system handles data collection, scoring, and structuring. The AI assistant handles interpretation and plain-English reasoning. You make the final call.

How to Access It

Open any run entry on the Archive page. Click the blue ⊙ Copy Prompt button to copy the full prompt to clipboard. Or click ↗ View Full Report to open the complete report page — it includes all candidate cards and the same copy button at the bottom.

What the Prompt Contains

Rules block
Timing rules, decision hierarchy, BUY NOW gate, conflict definitions, confidence scale — pre-loaded so the AI reasons consistently every time.
System vocabulary
All signal labels and their exact meanings so the AI interprets RESEARCH NOW, SUPPORT, ALIGNED, LATE TREND etc. correctly.
Global context
Date, slot, market regime, breadth, index values (Dow, S&P 500, Nasdaq), and 5-day commodity moves.
Per-candidate data
Price, confidence, trend (1M/3M/6M), volume, EQ score and tier, rotation score and signal, combined reading, strengths, and warnings.

What the AI Produces Per Candidate

1. Quick DecisionBUY NOW / WAIT / AVOID, timing quality classification, confidence in timing (2 sentences).
2. Why (Plain English)Trend condition, sector confirmation or contradiction, signal conflicts — no jargon.
3. Entry LogicIdeal scenario, bad scenario, what to wait for.
4. Exit LogicTake profit condition, cut loss condition — both in plain English.
5. Top Risks2–3 biggest risks specific to this candidate's data — no generic risks.
6. VerdictWorth researching further / Watch closely / Avoid for now.
7. Entry UrgencyImmediate / Near-term / Wait for confirmation, with a specific condition.

After all candidates, the AI adds a cross-candidate summary: best entry opportunity, worst timing, and overall market read.

Hard Rules Enforced in the Prompt

BUY NOW is only allowed if ALL of the following are true: - Timing = EARLY TREND or MID TREND - Market verdict = RESEARCH NOW - Rotation = SUPPORT - No major signal conflicts LATE TREND override requires ALL of: - Market verdict = RESEARCH NOW - Rotation = SUPPORT with score 80+ - No major contradictions Even then, maximum entry is WAIT — never BUY NOW. UNAVAILABLE earnings = reduced conviction. BUY NOW blocked unless all other signals are strong and timing is EARLY or MID TREND. Major conflict = any of: - Market verdict SKIP vs Rotation SUPPORT - Commodity trend contradicts sector direction - Earnings FAIL or RISKY with strong price trend

07 — The Archive

The Archive page stores every run the system has produced, organised by week. Each week is a collapsible section. Each run shows the timestamp, slot, candidate count, verdict counts (RESEARCH NOW / WATCH / SKIP), and market regime.

Expanding a run shows the candidate table with ticker, sector, confidence, and all three system verdicts, followed by the AI prompt copy button and a link to the full report.

Run Types

SCHEDULED Automatic run at one of the four daily slots.
MANUAL RUN User-triggered run on specific tickers for research. Tagged with a purple badge. Useful for investigating stocks outside the normal pipeline.

Retention

Up to 10 runs per week, 12 weeks of history. Full report files in docs/reports/ are pruned after 90 days by the weekly cleanup script every Sunday at 23:00 UTC.

08 — What This System Cannot Do

Free-tier data ceiling Free-tier APIs provide delayed, sampled, or limited-depth data. This system cannot access real-time tick data, institutional order flow, insider filings, earnings call transcripts, Level 2 order books, or premium fundamental databases. All values are best-effort approximations that may be stale by minutes to hours.
No predictions This system cannot predict whether a stock will go up or down. It identifies candidates that pass quantitative thresholds and organises available data for human review. Past signals do not indicate future performance.
EDGAR coverage gaps System 2 requires a minimum of 6 quarters of filed XBRL data from SEC EDGAR. Many tickers — especially smaller companies, recent IPOs, or foreign-listed stocks — will show UNAVAILABLE. This is a data availability issue, not a signal about the company's quality.
Sector rotation history depth System 3 accuracy improves with more historical runs. Early in the system's life, sector momentum history may have only 1–2 data points, limiting rotation speed calculations. Signals become more reliable as the system accumulates weeks of data.
Not investment advice. Free-tier data only. No trades are executed by this system. Always verify findings through independent sources before making any financial decision.

METRIC GUIDE

Confidence Score Composite screening score 0-100. Not a profit probability. 85+ = strong signal · 70-84 = solid · 60-69 = screening only
ROIC Return on Invested Capital — profit generated per dollar invested in the business. Above 15% = excellent. Below 8% = weak.
Trend (1M/3M/6M) Price return over 1, 3, and 6 months. Positive across all three = confirmed momentum. One timeframe only = early stage.
Signal Agreement How many independent signals point the same direction. Multiplicative — one weak signal pulls the whole score down.
Beta (Volatility) Market baseline = 1.0. Beta 1.8 = stock moves ~1.8x as much as the market. Higher beta = larger swings in both directions.
Drawdown How far the stock has fallen from its 90-day highest price. Above 20% = meaningful risk even if fundamentals look clean.
FCF Free Cash Flow — real cash the business generates after all expenses. Declining FCF is an early warning sign.
Risk Score Composite risk measure 0-100. Higher = more risk. Factors: debt, volatility, liquidity, earnings stability, drawdown.
